# Who is affected by the Cyber Resilience Act? Scope of Regulation (EU) 2024/2847

## Core principle: Making available on the EU market
    
        The CRA applies to all products with digital elements made available on the EU market,
        regardless of where they are manufactured (Art.2 Abs.1 CRA).
    

    
        Non-EU manufacturers exporting products into the EU are also in scope.
        It applies to hardware and software. Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) is covered ONLY
        when it is essential for the core function of a product.
    

    ## The four economic operators
    
        
            
                
                    Actor
                    Definition (Art.)
                    Core obligations
                
            
            
                
                    **Manufacturer**
                    Art.3 Nr.13: develops/manufactures a product or has it developed
                    Primary responsible party: Art.13 (security), Art.14 (reporting), CE marking
                
                
                    **Authorised representative**
                    Art.3 Nr.17: established in the EU, mandated by the manufacturer
                    Art.15 CRA: keep DoC and technical documentation available
                
                
                    **Importer**
                    Art.3 Nr.18: places a product from a third country on the EU market
                    Art.19 CRA: verify conformity, ensure CE marking is present
                
                
                    **Distributor**
                    Art.3 Nr.19: makes a product available on the EU market without being manufacturer/importer
                    Art.20 CRA: verify CE marking and documentation
                
            
        
    

    ## Exempt product areas (Art.2 Abs.2 CRA)
    
        - **Medical devices:** Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (MDR) and 2017/746 (IVDR)
        - **Motor vehicles:** Regulation (EU) 2019/2144
        - **Aviation:** Regulation (EU) 2018/1139
        - **Marine equipment:** Directive 2014/90/EU
        - **National security / military:** Art.2 Abs.3 CRA
    
    
        **Note:** These exemptions apply only to products fully covered by the listed sectoral regulation.
        Hybrid products may still partially fall under the CRA.
    

    ## Open-source software — special rule
    
        - **Commercial open-source software:** CRA applies in full.
        - 
            **Non-commercial open-source software:** CRA does NOT apply (Art.2 Abs.4 CRA),
            provided there is no direct or indirect economic compensation.
        
        - 
            **Open-source software steward (Art.3 Nr.14 CRA):**
            new economic-operator role with limited obligations (security policy, cooperation with authorities).
        
    
    
        **Note:** The commercial vs non-commercial distinction is not always clear in practice
        and is subject to ongoing discussion in the open-source community.
    

    ## Micro-enterprises and SMEs — relief measures
    Definition under EU Recommendation 2003/361/EC:

    
        - **Micro-enterprise:** < 10 employees AND turnover/balance sheet total ≤ EUR 2 million
        - **Small enterprise:** < 50 employees AND turnover/balance sheet total ≤ EUR 10 million
        - **OR logic:** turnover OR balance sheet total — both do not need to be met
    
    Relief measures for micro and small enterprises:

    
        - No fines for missing the 24h reporting deadline (Art.64 Abs.10 CRA)
        - Simplified access to regulatory sandboxes (Art.47 CRA)
        - Member States must provide support (Art.46 CRA)
    
    
        **Important:** All substantive CRA obligations apply IN FULL to SMEs as well.
