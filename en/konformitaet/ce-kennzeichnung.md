# CE Marking under the CRA: Requirements and Procedures

## What does CE marking mean under the CRA?
    
        - CE = Conformité Européenne: the manufacturer declares conformity with all applicable EU legislation.
        - Under the CRA, CE marking shows that the product meets the security requirements in Annex I.
        - Legal basis: Art.28-31 CRA.
        - Applies from: 11 December 2027 for all new products.
        - **IMPORTANT:** CE marking is mandatory. No product may bear CE without completed conformity assessment.
    

    ## Prerequisites for affixing the CE mark
    
        - Perform a cybersecurity risk analysis (Art.13 Abs.2 CRA).
        - 
            Classify the product (Default/Class I/II/Critical):
            [ /help/cra-grundlagen/produktklassifizierung](/help/cra-grundlagen/produktklassifizierung)
        
        - 
            Choose the applicable conformity assessment procedure:
            [ /help/konformitaet/konformitaetsbewertung](/help/konformitaet/konformitaetsbewertung)
        
        - Prepare technical documentation (Annex VII CRA).
        - 
            Create an SBOM (Annex I Part II):
            [ /help/sbom/sbom-anforderungen](/help/sbom/sbom-anforderungen)
        
        - Issue the EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC) (Art.28 CRA).
        - Affix the CE mark to the product or accompanying documentation (Art.30 CRA).
    

    ## The EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC)
    Mandatory content under Art.28 Abs.2 CRA:

    
        - Name and address of the manufacturer
        - Product identification (trade name, model, type, serial number)
        - Statement of the manufacturer's sole responsibility
        - Object of the declaration (product with digital elements)
        - Reference to applied harmonised standards or common specifications
        - Where applicable: name and identification number of the Notified Body
        - Place, date, signature
        - Retention: at least 10 years (Art.28 Abs.3 CRA)
    

    ## Affixing the CE mark (Art.30 CRA)
    
        - On the product itself or on packaging or accompanying documents (if physical affixing is not possible).
        - Minimum size: 5 mm height (except for very small products).
        - No other marks that could be confused with CE.
        - With a Notified Body: add the four-digit identification number after the CE mark.
    

    ## Market surveillance and consequences
    
        - National market surveillance authorities (in Germany: BSI) monitor CE conformity (Art.35 CRA).
        - False CE marking: fines up to EUR 15 million or 2.5% annual turnover (Art.64 Abs.1 CRA).
        - Product recall is possible in case of serious deficiencies.
        - **Note:** Enforcement practice varies between Member States.
