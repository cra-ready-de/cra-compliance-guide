# CRA Conformity Assessment Procedures: Which Route for Which Product?

## Overview — three routes to CE marking
    
        Conformity assessment is the proof that a product meets the security requirements in Annex I.
        The result is the EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC) and CE marking.
    

    ## Route 1 — Self-assessment (Module A)
    
        - 
            Applies to the default category (all products not listed in Annex III/IV)
            and to Class I when harmonised standards are fully applied.
        
        - Legal basis: Art.32 Abs.1 CRA, Annex VIII Module A.
        - 
            The manufacturer prepares: technical documentation (Annex VII),
            internal risk analysis and EU Declaration of Conformity.
        
    
    
        **Note:** Harmonised standards (Mandate M/606) are still under development.
        Target date for publication in the Official Journal: Q2 2027.
        Until then, BSI TR-03183 can be used for orientation.
    

    ## Route 2 — Notified Body (third-party assessment)
    
        - Mandatory for Class I (without harmonised standards), Class II (always), critical products.
        - Legal basis: Art.32 Abs.2 and Abs.3, Art.33, Art.34 CRA.
        - 
            The Notified Body reviews: technical documentation, processes,
            security architecture and vulnerability management.
        
    
    Status of Notified Bodies (May 2026):

    
        - 11 June 2026: Member States publish assessment procedures (Art.35 CRA).
        - 11 December 2026: Sufficient Notified Bodies should be available (Art.35 Abs.2 CRA).
        - 
            NANDO database: official list of designated bodies
            [https://ec.europa.eu/growth/tools-databases/nando/](https://ec.europa.eu/growth/tools-databases/nando/)
        
    

    ## Route 3 — EU cybersecurity certification
    
        - Alternative to Notified Body for Class II and critical products.
        - Legal basis: Art.32 Abs.4, Art.33 Abs.2, Art.34 Abs.3 CRA.
        - Basis: EU Cybersecurity Act (Regulation (EU) 2019/881) certification schemes.
        - 
            Requirement: the Commission must specify by delegated act
            which certification schemes satisfy CRA requirements.
        
        - Status: not yet finalised (as of May 2026).
    

    ## Technical documentation — content under Annex VII CRA
    
        - General description of the product
        - Cybersecurity risk analysis (Art.13 Abs.2 CRA)
        - Justification of product classification
        - SBOM (Software Bill of Materials)
        - Description of security architecture
        - Vulnerability handling processes
        - Declared support period (at least 5 years)
        - Description of secure supply chain
    

    ## Retention obligations
    
        - Technical documentation: at least 10 years after placing on the market or end of support (Art.13 Abs.14 CRA)
        - EU Declaration of Conformity: also 10 years
